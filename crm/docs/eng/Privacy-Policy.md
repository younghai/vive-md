## 2. Items of Personal Information Collected and Collection Methods

### 2.1 Personal Information We Collect

#### Items Collected During Sign-Up (Required)

| Item | Purpose | Retention Period |
|------|---------|------------------|
| Email | Member identification, login, service notices | 30 days after account deletion |
| Password | Member authentication | Immediately destroyed upon account deletion |
| Name | Display within the service | 30 days after account deletion |

| Item | Purpose | Retention Period |
|------|---------|------------------|

| Item | Purpose | Retention Period |
|------|---------|------------------|

| Payment date and amount | Transaction records | 5 years |
| Invoice issuance information | Tax filing | 5 years |

### 2.2 How Personal Information Is Collected

- By completing the website sign-up form
- Automatically generated and collected during service use
- Through customer inquiries or event participation
- From external systems when using API integrations

### 2.3 Customer Information Registered by Members

VIVE CRM is a **customer relationship management service**, and Members may register and manage their own customer information. In such cases, Members must comply with the following:

- **Consent to collection:** Members must obtain consent from their customers before registering personal information
- **Responsibility:** Members are responsible for collecting, using, and managing customer information that they register
- **Entrusted processing:** The Company acts as a processor for the customer information entrusted by Members

## 4. Retention and Use Period of Personal Information

### 4.1 Retention Principle

The Company destroys personal information without delay once the purpose of collection and use has been fulfilled.

### 4.2 Exceptions

However, personal information may be retained in the following cases:

| Reason | Relevant Law | Retention Period |
|--------|--------------|------------------|
| Commercial record retention obligation | Commercial Act | 10 years |
| E-commerce records | Act on Consumer Protection in Electronic Commerce | 5 years |
| Records on payment and supply of goods/services | Electronic Commerce Act | 5 years |
| Records on consumer complaints or dispute resolution | Electronic Commerce Act | 3 years |
| Records on display and advertising | Electronic Commerce Act | 6 months |
| Website visit records | Protection of Communications Secrets Act | 3 months |

### 4.3 Dormant Account Handling

- Accounts with no login record for one year will be converted to dormant status
- Members will be notified by email 30 days before dormancy
- Even in dormant status, personal information is safely stored in encrypted form

## 5. Procedures and Methods for Destruction of Personal Information

### 5.1 Destruction Procedure

```text
[Purpose Fulfilled] → [Separated into a Separate DB] → [Retention Period Elapsed] → [Destruction Executed]
```

1. **Separated retention:** After the purpose is fulfilled, the data is separated into a dedicated DB for storage
2. **Restricted access:** Access rights to the separated personal information are restricted
3. **Destruction:** Once the retention period elapses, the data is destroyed immediately

### 5.2 Destruction Methods

| Information Type | Destruction Method |
|------------------|--------------------|
| Electronic files | Permanently deleted using an irreversible method such as data overwriting |
| Paper documents | Shredding or incineration |

## 8. Member Rights and How to Exercise Them

### 8.1 Member Rights

Members may exercise the following rights at any time:

| Right | Description | How to Exercise |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| Access | View their own personal information | "My Info" menu in the service or request by email |
| Correction | Correct inaccurate personal information | "Edit Profile" in the service or request by email |
| Deletion | Request deletion of personal information | Request account withdrawal by email |
| Suspension of processing | Temporarily suspend processing of personal information | Request by email (may be restricted if legal grounds apply) |
| Withdrawal of consent | Withdraw consent to collection and use of personal information | In-service settings or request by email |

### 8.2 How to Exercise Rights

- **Email:** `privacy@vive-crm.com`
- **Within the service:** `Settings > Privacy Management`

The Company will notify the Member of the processing result within 10 days after receiving the request. However, the exercise of rights may be limited if retention is required by other laws or if it is necessary to fulfill legal obligations.

### 8.3 Exercise by a Representative

A legal representative of a child under 14 years of age, or a person authorized by the Member, may exercise rights on behalf of the Member.

## 9. Measures to Ensure the Security of Personal Information

### 9.1 Technical Measures

| Measure | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Encryption | Sensitive information such as passwords and resident registration numbers is stored encrypted |
| Transmission encryption | All data transmission uses TLS 1.2 or higher |
| Access control | Database access permissions are minimized |
| Backup | Personal information is regularly backed up in encrypted form |
| Security software | Antivirus, firewall, and intrusion detection systems are operated |

### 9.2 Administrative Measures

| Measure | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Internal management plan | Establishment and execution of an internal management plan for personal information protection |
| Access rights management | Access rights to personal information are limited to the minimum necessary for job performance |
| Training | Regular training for employees who process personal information |
| Security pledge | Security pledge by employees handling personal information |

### 9.3 Physical Measures

- As a cloud-based service, no separate physical server room is operated
- Data centers provided by vendors with security certifications such as ISO 27001 and SOC 2 are used
